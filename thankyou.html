Debriefing Form

The study in which you just participated was designed to measure the effects of self-control in athletes versus non-athletes, with an emphasis on endurance athletes. You were asked to complete a self-control survey to the best of your ability. Additionally, you were asked to complete a questionnaire that involved applications of self-control in other areas of your life such as procrastination and diet. We will be testing the effects of athletic participation on overall self-control ratings compared to the self-control ratings of non-athletes. Furthermore, we will test if self-control in athletes is implemented throughout various aspects of their life.

Please do not discuss this study with other potential participants until the semester is over. If people know what we are testing before the study begins, they may respond differently, risking our results.

If you would like a report on the results of the research, you can email Bill Altermatt at altermattw@hanover.edu after April 15, 2016. If you have any questions, please contact:

  • For questions about the research itself, you may contact the researchers: Jake Hally at hallyj@hanover.edu and Brittany Ferrell at ferrellb17@hanover.edu.
  • For questions about your rights as a participant in this research, you may contact the faculty member supervising the research, Dr. Bill Altermatt, at altermattw@hanover.edu, or the chair of Hanover College’s Institutional Review Board, Dean Jacks, at jacks@hanover.edu.

For more information on these topics, we suggest reading Roy Baumeister and John Tierney’s 2011 book, Willpower: Rediscovering the Greatest Human Strength.
